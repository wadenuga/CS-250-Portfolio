# CS-250-Portfolio Reflection
## How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How do user stories help with this? 

I interpret user needs by listening and understanding the problems users want to solve and translating those requirements into practical software features. This involves listening carefully, asking clarifying questions, and ensuring the final solution aligns with user expectations. User stories support this process by describing features from the user's perspective, making it easier to understand their goals and priorities. They also help the development team stay focused on delivering features that provide the greatest value. 

## How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work? 

I approach software development by dividing large projects into smaller, manageable tasks and testing each component throughout the development process. This course reinforced the importance of Agile practices such as sprint planning, maintaining a product backlog, holding daily stand-up meetings, and conducting sprint reviews and retrospectives. I plan to continue using these Agile methods because they improve organization, encourage collaboration, and make it easier to respond to changing requirements. 

## What does it mean to be a good team member in software development? 

Being a good team member means communicating effectively, collaborating with others, and taking responsibility for completing assigned tasks on time. It also involves being open to constructive feedback, supporting teammates when needed, and contributing to shared project goals. By demonstrating respect, accountability, and a willingness to learn, team members help create a productive and successful software development environment.
